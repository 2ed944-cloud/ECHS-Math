2.1 Rates of Change and Tangent Lines

Average rate of change, secant slope, instantaneous rate of change, and the tangent-slope limit.

Teacher: Mohammad Abu Ghuwaleh

Learning objectives

  • Determine average rates of change using difference quotients.
  • Use limits to move from rates over intervals to rates at an instant.
  • Represent the derivative at a point as the limit of a difference quotient.
  • Connect secant slopes, tangent slopes, and units in context.
Opening

Do Now: interval rate or instant rate?

A

A car travels from 120 km to 180 km between 2:00 and 3:30.

Find the rate that can be determined exactly.

B

A speedometer reads 72 km/h exactly at 2:15.

What kind of rate is being described?

A: \(\dfrac{180-120}{1.5}=40\) km/h, an average rate. B: an instantaneous rate at one time.

Idea

Average rate of change

For points \(P(a,f(a))\) and \(Q(b,f(b))\), the average rate of change on \([a,b]\) is the slope of the secant line.

\[\operatorname{AROC}_{[a,b]}=\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\]

Units: output units per input unit.

From interval to instant

Secant slopes approaching one point

Let the second point move closer to \(x=a\).

\[P=(a,f(a)),\qquad Q=(a+h,f(a+h))\]
\[m_{\text{sec}}=\frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}\]
Instantaneous rate

The tangent-slope limit

If the secant slopes approach one number as \(h\to0\), that number is the instantaneous rate of change at \(x=a\).

\[m_{\text{tan}}=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}\]

Equivalent form:

\[m_{\text{tan}}=\lim_{x\to a}\frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}\]

Both forms represent the same tangent slope when the limit exists.

Notation

Build the difference quotient

For each function, write \(f(a)\) and \(f(a+h)\).

1. \(f(x)=2x\)

2. \(f(x)=3x-2\)

3. \(f(x)=2x-x^2\)

4. \(f(x)=\dfrac{3}{x+1}\)

1) \(2a,2a+2h\). 2) \(3a-2,3a+3h-2\). 3) \(2a-a^2,2(a+h)-(a+h)^2\). 4) \(\frac{3}{a+1},\frac{3}{a+h+1}\).

Worked example

Instantaneous rate using the \(h\)-form

Find the instantaneous rate of change of \(f(x)=x^2\) at \(x=3\).

\[\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(3+h)-f(3)}{h}=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{(3+h)^2-9}{h}\]

\[=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{6h+h^2}{h}=\lim_{h\to0}(6+h)=6.\]

Worked example

Use both limit forms

Find the instantaneous rate of change of \(f(x)=x-x^2\) at \(x=-1\) using both forms.

Using \(h\)

\[\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(-1+h)-f(-1)}{h}\]

Using \(x\)

\[\lim_{x\to-1}\frac{f(x)-f(-1)}{x+1}\]

\(f(-1)=-2\). Both forms simplify to the same value: \(3\).

Existence

When the tangent slope does not exist

A tangent slope may fail because of a corner, discontinuity, vertical tangent, or unstable left/right behavior.

Simple tangent line connection

Use a known slope to write a line

1. A curve passes through \((3,9)\), and its tangent slope at that point is \(6\). Write the tangent line in point-slope form.

2. For \(f(x)=x^2\), the tangent slope at \(x=2\) is \(4\). Write the tangent line at \(x=2\).

1) \(y-9=6(x-3)\). 2) Since \(f(2)=4\), \(y-4=4(x-2)\).

Error analysis

Find and fix the error

A student tries to find the instantaneous rate for \(f(x)=x^2-x\) at \(x=-1\):

\[\frac{f(-1+h)-f(-1)}{h}=\frac{(-1+h)^2-(-1+h)-(-1)^2-(-1)}{h}\]

Then the student cancels \(h\) from the denominator immediately.

Explain the error and complete the problem correctly.

The numerator must be simplified first; you may cancel only a common factor. The numerator becomes \(-3h+h^2\), so the quotient is \(-3+h\), and the limit is \(-3\).

Challenge

Build a function from rate information

A quadratic function \(s(t)=at^2+bt+c\) has average rate of change \(8\) on \([1,3]\) and instantaneous rate of change \(10\) at \(t=3\). Find one possible pair \(a,b\).

Use \(\frac{s(3)-s(1)}{2}=8\) and \(\lim_{h\to0}\frac{s(3+h)-s(3)}{h}=10\).

For \(s(t)=at^2+bt+c\), average rate on \([1,3]\) is \(4a+b\), and instantaneous rate at \(3\) is \(6a+b\). So \(4a+b=8\), \(6a+b=10\), giving \(a=1\), \(b=4\). Any \(c\) works.
