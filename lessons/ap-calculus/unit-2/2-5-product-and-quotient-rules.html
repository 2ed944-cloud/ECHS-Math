Product and Quotient Rules

Differentiate products and quotients, decide when rewriting is better, and use derivatives in tangent-line, table, and AP-style questions.

AP Calculus AB

Do not multiply derivatives

Incorrect: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}[f(x)g(x)] = f'(x)g'(x)\)
Correct: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}[f(x)g(x)] = f'(x)g(x)+f(x)g'(x)\)
Test the false rule using \(f(x)=x^2\) and \(g(x)=x^3\). Compare the derivative of \(x^5\) with \((2x)(3x^2)\).
True derivative: \(5x^4\). False result: \(6x^3\). They are not the same.

Product Rule

If \(u\) and \(v\) are differentiable, then \[ \frac{d}{dx}[u(x)v(x)] = u'(x)v(x)+u(x)v'(x). \]
StepAction
1Identify \(u\) and \(v\).
2Compute \(u'\) and \(v'\).
3Form \(u'v+uv'\).
4Simplify only if it helps.

Quotient Rule

If \(u\) and \(v\) are differentiable and \(v(x)\ne0\), then \[ \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{u(x)}{v(x)}\right] = \frac{u'(x)v(x)-u(x)v'(x)}{[v(x)]^2}. \]
Low times derivative of high minus high times derivative of low, over low squared.

Tangent lines with product and quotient rules

At \(x=a\), use \(y-f(a)=f'(a)(x-a)\).
1. \(f(x)=(x^3+1)(x^2-2x+1)\), \(a=-1\).
2. \(g(x)=\dfrac{x^2-9}{x-2}\), \(a=1\).

Horizontal tangents

A horizontal tangent occurs where \(f'(x)=0\) and the derivative exists.
1. Find all horizontal tangents of \(f(x)=x^3-3x+1\).
2. Find all horizontal tangents of \(g(x)=\dfrac{x}{x^2+1}\).
3. Find all \(x\)-values where \(h(x)=x^2e^x\) has a horizontal tangent.
4. Find all \(x\)-values where \(p(x)=\dfrac{x^2-1}{x^2+1}\) has a horizontal tangent.
1. \(x=\pm1\). 2. \(g'(x)=\frac{1-x^2}{(x^2+1)^2}\), so \(x=\pm1\). 3. \(h'=e^x(x^2+2x)=e^xx(x+2)\), so \(x=0,-2\). 4. \(p'=\frac{4x}{(x^2+1)^2}\), so \(x=0\).

Normal lines

A normal line is perpendicular to the tangent line. If \(m_{\text{tan}}\ne0\), then \(m_{\text{normal}}=-\frac1{m_{\text{tan}}}\).
1. Find the tangent and normal lines to \(f(x)=3\sqrt{x}+4\) at \(x=4\).
2. Find the tangent and normal lines to \(y=\frac{x^2}{2}+\frac32x-2\) at \(x=8\).
1. \(f(4)=10\), \(f'(4)=\frac34\). Tangent: \(y-10=\frac34(x-4)\). Normal: \(y-10=-\frac43(x-4)\). 2. \(y(8)=42\), \(y'=x+\frac32\), slope \(\frac{19}{2}\). Tangent: \(y-42=\frac{19}{2}(x-8)\). Normal: \(y-42=-\frac{2}{19}(x-8)\).

Product Rule proof challenge

Prove the Product Rule using the definition of derivative. Start with \[ \frac{f(x+h)g(x+h)-f(x)g(x)}{h}. \] Add and subtract \(f(x+h)g(x)\) in the numerator.
\[ \frac{f(x+h)[g(x+h)-g(x)]}{h}+g(x)\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}. \] As \(h\to0\), \(f(x+h)\to f(x)\) since differentiability implies continuity, so the limit is \(f(x)g'(x)+g(x)f'(x)\).

Quotient Rule proof challenge

Assume \(g(x)\ne0\). Use the Product Rule on \[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}=f(x)[g(x)]^{-1} \] to prove the Quotient Rule.
\[ \left(fg^{-1}\right)'=f'g^{-1}+f(-1)g^{-2}g'=\frac{f'}g-\frac{fg'}{g^2}=\frac{f'g-fg'}{g^2}. \]

Find a missing derivative value

Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable. Suppose \[ f(2)=3,\quad g(2)=-1,\quad f'(2)=5, \] and \[ \lim_{x\to2}\frac{f(x)g(x)+3}{x-2}=11. \] Find \(g'(2)\).

Quotient limit challenge

Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable at \(x=1\), where \[ f(1)=2,\quad g(1)=4,\quad f'(1)=3. \] Suppose \[ \lim_{x\to1}\frac{\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}-\frac12}{x-1}=1. \] Find \(g'(1)\).

Very difficult challenge

Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable at \(x=a\), with \(g(a)\ne0\). Suppose \[ \lim_{x\to a}\frac{f(x)g(a)-f(a)g(x)}{x-a}=12. \] Express the limit in terms of \(f(a),g(a),f'(a),g'(a)\), then connect it to \(\left(\frac fg\right)'(a)\).

Product and quotient limits I

1. Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable at \(x=1\), where \(f(1)=2\) and \(g(1)=-3\). Suppose \[ \lim_{x\to1}\frac{f(x)g(x)+6}{x-1}=11 \] and \[ \lim_{x\to1}\frac{\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}+\frac23}{x-1}=-\frac59. \] Find \(f'(1)\) and \(g'(1)\).
2. Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable at \(x=2\), where \(g(2)\ne0\). Suppose \(f(2)=g(2)\), \((fg)'(2)=24\), and \(\left(\frac fg\right)'(2)=\frac12\). Find \(f'(2)\) and \(g'(2)\) in terms of \(g(2)\).
1. \(-3f'(1)+2g'(1)=11\) and \(\frac{-3f'(1)-2g'(1)}9=-\frac59\). Hence \(f'(1)=-1\) and \(g'(1)=4\).
2. Let \(m=g(2)=f(2)\). Then \(f'(2)+g'(2)=\frac{24}{m}\) and \(f'(2)-g'(2)=\frac{m}{2}\). Thus \(f'(2)=\frac{12}{m}+\frac{m}{4}\), \(g'(2)=\frac{12}{m}-\frac{m}{4}\).

Zero value shortcuts

7. Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable at \(x=2\), where \(f(2)=0\), \(g(2)=5\), and \(g'(2)=7\). Suppose \[ \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(2+h)g(2+h)}{h}=30. \] Find \(f'(2)\).
8. Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable at \(x=1\), where \(f(1)=0\), \(g(1)=-4\), and \(g'(1)=3\). Suppose \[ \lim_{h\to0}\frac{\frac{f(1+h)}{g(1+h)}}{h}=5. \] Find \(f'(1)\).
7. Since \((fg)(2)=0\), the limit is \((fg)'(2)=f'(2)g(2)+f(2)g'(2)=5f'(2)\). Thus \(f'(2)=6\).
8. Since \(f(1)=0\), \(\left(\frac fg\right)'(1)=\frac{f'(1)g(1)}{[g(1)]^2}=-\frac{f'(1)}4=5\). Thus \(f'(1)=-20\).
Challenge Questions

Quotient parameters and tangent lines

9. Let \(f(x)=\dfrac{ax+b}{cx+d}\), where \(c\ne0\) and \(cx+d\ne0\). Find the condition on \(a,b,c,d\) so that \(f'(x)=0\) for every \(x\) in the domain. Explain why the function is constant on each interval of its domain.
10. Let \(f(x)=\dfrac{x^2+a}{x+b}\). Find all values of \(a\) and \(b\) such that the tangent line to \(f\) at \(x=1\) is \(y=-x+5\).
11. Let \(f(x)=\dfrac{x^2+ax+b}{x+1}\). Find all values of \(a\) and \(b\) such that \(f(2)=5\) and \(f\) has a horizontal tangent at \(x=2\).
9. \(f'(x)=\frac{ad-bc}{(cx+d)^2}\), so \(ad-bc=0\). Then the numerator is a constant multiple of the denominator, so the function is constant on each interval of its domain.
10. \(f(1)=4\Rightarrow a=3+4b\), and \(f'(1)=-1\Rightarrow b=1\), so \(a=7\).
11. \(2a+b=11\) and \(8+a-b=0\). Hence \(a=1\), \(b=9\).
Challenge Questions

Removable quotients

12. Let \[ f(x)=\frac{x^3+ax^2+bx+c}{x-1},\qquad x\ne1. \] Find \(a,b,c\) such that \(f\) has a removable discontinuity at \(x=1\), and the simplified function has derivative \(6\) at \(x=1\).
13. Let \[ f(x)=\frac{x^3+ax^2+bx+c}{x-2},\qquad x\ne2. \] Find \(a,b,c\) such that \(f\) has a removable discontinuity at \(x=2\), and the continuous extension has tangent line \(y=4x-1\) at \(x=2\).
12. Dividing by \(x-1\) gives \(x^2+(a+1)x+(b+a+1)\) with remainder \(a+b+c+1\). The derivative at \(1\) is \(a+3\), so \(a=3\), and the removable condition gives \(b+c=-4\).
13. Dividing by \(x-2\) gives \(x^2+(a+2)x+(b+2a+4)\). The tangent line requires value \(7\) and slope \(4\) at \(x=2\). Thus \(a=-2\), \(b=3\), and the removable condition gives \(c=-6\).
Challenge Questions

Extensions and original domains

14. Let \[ f(x)=\frac{x^4-1}{x^2-1}. \] The original function is undefined at \(x=1\) and \(x=-1\). Simplify \(f(x)\) where possible, then determine whether the continuous extension has the same derivative at \(x=1\) and \(x=-1\).
15. Let \[ f(x)=\frac{x^3-27}{x^2-9}. \] Find the domain of the original function. Simplify where possible and determine whether \(f'(3)\) exists for the original function and for the continuous extension.
16. Let \(f(x)=\dfrac{x^2+a}{x^2+b}\). Find all values of \(a\) and \(b\) such that \(f(1)=2\) and \(f\) has a horizontal tangent at \(x=1\).
14. For \(x\ne\pm1\), \(f(x)=x^2+1\). The extension has derivative \(2x\), so the derivatives are \(2\) at \(x=1\) and \(-2\) at \(x=-1\); they are not the same.
15. Original domain: \(x\ne -3,3\). For \(x\ne\pm3\), \(f(x)=\frac{x^2+3x+9}{x+3}\). The original \(f'(3)\) does not exist because \(f(3)\) is undefined. The extension derivative at \(3\) is \(\frac34\).
16. No solution. The equations force \(a=b=-1\), but then \(f(1)\) is undefined.

Challenge Questions

Derivative information from products and quotients

21. Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable at \(x=a\), with \(g(a)\ne0\). Suppose \[ \lim_{x\to a}\frac{f(x)g(a)-f(a)g(x)}{x-a}=15. \] Express \(\left(\frac fg\right)'(a)\) in terms of \(g(a)\).
22. Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable at \(x=2\), where \(f(2)=4\), \(g(2)=5\), and \(g'(2)=3\). Suppose \(\left(\frac fg\right)'(2)=2\). Find \(f'(2)\).
23. Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable at \(x=-1\), where \(f(-1)=6\), \(g(-1)=-2\), and \(f'(-1)=5\). Suppose \((fg)'(-1)=1\). Find \(g'(-1)\).
21. The numerator limit is \(g(a)f'(a)-f(a)g'(a)=15\), so \(\left(\frac fg\right)'(a)=\frac{15}{[g(a)]^2}\).
22. \(2=\frac{5f'(2)-4(3)}{25}\), so \(f'(2)=\frac{62}{5}\).
23. \(1=5(-2)+6g'(-1)\), so \(g'(-1)=\frac{11}{6}\).

Symmetric product limits

24. Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable at \(x=1\), where \(f(1)=2\) and \(g(1)=3\). Evaluate the limit in terms of \(f'(1)\) and \(g'(1)\): \[ \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(1+h)g(1-h)-f(1-h)g(1+h)}{2h}. \]
25. Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable at \(x=0\), where \(f(0)=A\) and \(g(0)=B\). Find a formula for \[ \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(\alpha h)g(\beta h)-f(\gamma h)g(\delta h)}{h} \] in terms of \(A,B,f'(0),g'(0),\alpha,\beta,\gamma,\delta\).
24. The limit is \(3f'(1)-2g'(1)\).
25. The limit is \((\alpha-\gamma)Bf'(0)+(\beta-\delta)Ag'(0)\).

Exit ticket

1. Differentiate \(y=(x^2+3)\ln x\), \(x>0\).
2. Differentiate \(y=\dfrac{x^2+1}{e^x}\).
3. Find the tangent line to \(f(x)=x^2\sin x\) at \(x=\pi\).
4. Let \(h(x)=\dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}\). If \(f(2)=6\), \(f'(2)=1\), \(g(2)=3\), and \(g'(2)=-4\), find \(h'(2)\).
1. \(2x\ln x+\frac{x^2+3}{x}\). 2. Rewrite \((x^2+1)e^{-x}\) or quotient: \(e^{-x}(2x-x^2-1)\). 3. \(f(\pi)=0\), \(f'(x)=2x\sin x+x^2\cos x\), \(f'(\pi)=-\pi^2\), so \(y=-\pi^2(x-\pi)\). 4. \(\frac{1(3)-6(-4)}{9}=3\).
