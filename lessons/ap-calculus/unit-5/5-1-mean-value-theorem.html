Mean Value Theorem

Average rate, instantaneous rate, Rolle’s Theorem, hypotheses, tables, motion, proof questions, and AP-style challenges.

Essential Claim

If ff is continuous on [a,b][a,b] and differentiable on (a,b)(a,b), then for some c∈(a,b)c\in(a,b),

f′(c)=f(b)−f(a)b−a.f'(c)=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}.

Graph Meaning

At least one tangent line inside the interval is parallel to the secant line through the endpoints.

Lesson Focus

Learning Objectives

  • Justify conclusions by applying the Mean Value Theorem over an interval.
  • Check continuity on the closed interval and differentiability on the open interval.
  • Find all possible values of cc where instantaneous rate equals average rate.
  • Use MVT and Rolle’s Theorem in tables, graphs, proofs, roots, uniqueness, fixed points, and motion.

Concept Check

MVT vs IVT

Mean Value Theorem

Guarantees a derivative value:

f′(c)=f(b)−f(a)b−a.f'(c)=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}.

It is about a slope.

Intermediate Value Theorem

Guarantees a function value between endpoint function values.

It is about a yy-value.

Special Case

Rolle’s Theorem

If f(a)=f(b)f(a)=f(b), the secant slope is 00. Find where the tangent is horizontal.
Rolle’s Theorem is the MVT when f(a)=f(b)f(a)=f(b), so f′(c)=0f'(c)=0.
Before Applying MVT

Hypothesis Checklist

1. State the interval [a,b][a,b].
2. Check continuity on every point of [a,b][a,b].
3. Check differentiability on (a,b)(a,b), not at endpoints.
4. Compute the average rate.
5. Solve f′(c)=f(b)−f(a)b−af'(c)=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}, and keep only c∈(a,b)c\in(a,b).

Which statement is always true? A continuous function on [a,b][a,b] that is differentiable on (a,b)(a,b) must have: (A) a zero, (B) a critical point, (C) a tangent parallel to the secant line, (D) equal endpoint values.
Choice C. This is exactly the Mean Value Theorem.
Teacher Check

Common Mistakes

Do not apply MVT before checking hypotheses.
Do not require endpoint differentiability.
Do not confuse IVT conclusions with MVT conclusions.
Do not stop after finding one cc if the question asks for all values.
Do not assume failure of a hypothesis means the conclusion is false.
Final Check

Exit Ticket

Create one example where MVT applies and one example where the conclusion happens to be true even though a hypothesis fails. Explain the difference.
The key distinction is between a theorem being applicable and the conclusion being accidentally true.
