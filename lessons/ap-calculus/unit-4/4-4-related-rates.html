4.4 Related Rates

4.4

One complete lesson: modeling, average rate, instantaneous rate, and applied related rates.

Learning targets
  • Translate situations into rate notation.
  • Build a relation between changing quantities.
  • Differentiate all variables with respect to time.
  • Interpret the sign, units, and reasonableness of the answer.

Visual audit: all interactive models use the same equations as the worked solutions; generic diagrams were replaced by context-specific figures.

Concept

The big idea

Related rates are implicit differentiation in context.

\[F(x(t),y(t),z(t),\ldots)=0\quad\Longrightarrow\quad {d\over dt}F(x(t),y(t),z(t),\ldots)=0\]
The chain rule is the reason every changing quantity gets its own rate, such as \(dx/dt\), \(dy/dt\), or \(dV/dt\).
Rate foundation

Average rate comes first

Average rate on an interval

\[\text{average rate}={Q(t_2)-Q(t_1)\over t_2-t_1}\]
This uses two snapshots. It does not require a derivative.

Average versus instantaneous rate

Show solution / setup
For example, if \(s(t)=t^2+3t\), then the average rate on \([1,4]\) is \((s(4)-s(1))/(4-1)=8\), while the instantaneous rate at \(t=4\) is \(s\prime(4)=11\).
Rate foundation

Instantaneous rate

Rate at an instant

\[Q'(t)=\lim_{\Delta t\to 0}{Q(t+\Delta t)-Q(t)\over \Delta t}\]
Most related-rates problems ask for an instantaneous rate after the quantities are related.
Method

Six-step routine

  1. Draw the situation and define variables.
  2. Write what is fixed and what is changing.
  3. List known rates and the requested rate.
  4. Write one equation connecting the variables.
  5. Differentiate with respect to time before substituting snapshot values.
  6. Solve, then interpret the sign and units.
Error clinic

Do not substitute too early

If a quantity changes, do not replace it with its snapshot value before differentiating.

Wrong

Use \(r=4\) before differentiating \(A=\pi r^2\).

Right

Differentiate first: \(A'=2\pi r r'\).

Then

Substitute \(r=4\) and the given rate.
