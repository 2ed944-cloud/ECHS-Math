What Changes Together?

A water tank starts with 12 liters. It is filled at a steady rate of 3 liters per minute.

Time \(t\)01234
Water \(W(t)\)1215182124

What changes? What stays consistent? What does the starting value represent?

For every 1-minute increase in time, the water amount increases by 3 liters. The two quantities vary together in a predictable way.

Misconception Check

Domain, Range, and Context

Pure math

For \(f(x)=x^2\), the domain is all real numbers and the range is \(y\ge0\).

Context

If \(x\) is time after a race starts, negative values may not make sense. The context can restrict the domain.

Turn and talk: How can a graph be mathematically correct but unrealistic for the situation?

Practice

Create a Model From a Verbal Description

A student’s phone battery begins at 84% and decreases by about 6% per hour.

  1. Make a table for 0–5 hours.
  2. Sketch a graph.
  3. Write a rule.
  4. State a reasonable domain and range.
A linear model is \(B(t)=84-6t\). For 0 to 5 hours, the battery goes from 84% to 54%. A reasonable domain could be \(0\le t\le 14\) if the model is used until the battery reaches 0%.
