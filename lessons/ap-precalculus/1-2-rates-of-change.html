Rates of Change

Learning target

Compare rates of change over intervals and near points, then connect rate of change to function behavior.

Average rate of change: \(\dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\)

Big idea

Rates of change describe how quickly the output changes relative to the input.

Warm-up

Same Change in x, Different Change in y

x01234
f(x)014916

For each one-unit increase in \(x\), how does \(f(x)\) change?

The outputs change by 1, 3, 5, and 7. The rate is increasing, which matches the concave-up behavior of \(f(x)=x^2\).
Concept

Average Rate of Change

Formula

\(\text{AROC}_{[a,b]}=\dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\)

This is the slope of the secant line through \((a,f(a))\) and \((b,f(b))\).

Worked Example

Compare Two Intervals

Let \(h(t)=16t^2\). Compare the average rate of change on \([1,2]\) and \([2,3]\).

\([1,2]:\ \dfrac{64-16}{1}=48\qquad [2,3]:\ \dfrac{144-64}{1}=80\)

The rate of change is larger on \([2,3]\), so the function is increasing faster later.

Precision

Near a Point

To estimate a rate of change near \(x=2\), use smaller intervals around the point.

IntervalAROC for \(f(x)=x^2\)
[2,3]5
[2,2.1]4.1
[2,2.01]4.01

This prepares students for the calculus idea of instantaneous rate of change.

Practice

Interpret in Context

A car’s distance from school is modeled by \(d(t)=5t^2+20\), where \(t\) is hours and \(d\) is kilometers.

  1. Find the average rate of change from \(t=1\) to \(t=3\).
  2. Interpret the answer with units.
  3. Explain whether the car is moving at a constant speed.
\(d(1)=25\), \(d(3)=65\), so AROC \(=(65-25)/(3-1)=20\) km/h. The model is quadratic, so the speed is not constant.
